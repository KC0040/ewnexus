You Own a Restaurant. 90% of Diners Check the Menu Online Before They Leave Home.

A family of four is deciding where to eat Friday night. The kids want something specific. The parents are trying to accommodate everyone. They open Google, search "Mexican restaurant near me," and start clicking through the top results.

They're not looking for a Yelp page. They're looking for a menu. The first restaurant that shows them a clear, attractive menu with photos wins the reservation.

If your restaurant doesn't have a website — or has one that looks like it was built in 2009 — they move on in 3 seconds and never come back.

What Restaurant Owners Deal With Every Day

❌ "Do you have a menu online?" You get this call 10 times a day. Someone wants to know what you serve before they come in. You don't have a website, so you try to describe the menu over the phone. They say "okay, thanks" and you never see them.

❌ Wrong hours, wrong address on Google. Without a website that you control, the information Google shows about your restaurant may be wrong — outdated hours, old phone number, incorrect address. Customers arrive when you're closed and don't come back.

❌ No reservations without a phone call. People who want to book a table for Saturday night don't want to call. They want to click a button. If you can't offer that, they book somewhere that can.

❌ Third-party delivery apps take 30%. Uber Eats and DoorDash are convenient, but they take 25–30% of every order. If you had your own website with a WhatsApp order option, you'd keep that margin yourself on direct orders.

What a Restaurant Website Fixes

✅ Menu online — searchable, shareable, always updated. Your full menu with photos and prices is visible to anyone on Google. When you add a special or change a dish, you update it in minutes. Customers see your best work before they decide.

✅ You control your information on Google. Your hours, address, phone number, and photos come from your website — which you own. No more wrong information from outdated third-party listings.

✅ Reservation form works 24/7. Someone at midnight decides to book a birthday dinner for next week. They go to your site, pick the date, party size, and any notes — and submit. You wake up with a reservation confirmed. No phone tag needed.

✅ Direct WhatsApp ordering for takeout. Add a "Order for Pickup" button that opens a WhatsApp conversation. Customer sends their order, you confirm, they pick it up. You keep 100% — no delivery app commission.

The SEO Angle for Restaurants

Restaurant searches are intensely local and intensely competitive. "Tacos near me," "best burger [city]," "Chinese food open now" — these searches happen millions of times per day. The restaurants that show up in Google's top results get the traffic, the reservations, and the walk-ins.

A properly set-up restaurant website is the foundation of showing up in those searches. It tells Google who you are, where you are, what you serve, and when you're open — all the signals Google needs to send people your way.

Restaurants that launch a basic website with proper SEO typically see a 20–35% increase in new customer volume within 90 days compared to operating with no web presence.

Food Trucks Too

Food truck owners: this applies even more to you. Your location changes. Your menu changes. Customers want to know where you'll be this weekend. A simple website — or even just a well-structured landing page — with your schedule and current location is something your Instagram followers have to actively seek out. With a website, anyone searching Google can find it.
