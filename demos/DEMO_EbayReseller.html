Common Questions

Consignment FAQ

Transparent answers before you drop off a single item. No surprises — ever.

How do you charge for consignment?
We charge 30% of the final sale price — no upfront listing fees. This is calculated from the net amount after eBay's platform fees are deducted. You'll see the full breakdown in your consignment agreement and in your weekly payout statement.
What items do you accept?
Electronics, collectibles, clothing, antiques, tools, and most household goods in good condition. Items should be clean, functional, and accurately described. We do not accept items that are broken, heavily damaged, recalled, or prohibited from sale on eBay. If you're unsure, bring it in — we'll tell you on the spot.
How long does it take to sell my items?
Most items sell within 7–21 days on eBay. High-demand electronics and collectibles often sell within the first few days. Specialty items or higher-priced goods may take a few weeks. We'll give you a realistic timeline estimate when you drop off, based on current market activity.
Who pays the eBay fees?
eBay charges ~12.9–15% in selling fees, which are deducted before we calculate your share. These are standard eBay marketplace fees — set by eBay Inc., not by us. The exact percentage varies by category. We show you the exact deduction on every payout statement.
What if my item doesn't sell?
We relist once at no extra charge, then return the item or donate per your preference. If an item repeatedly doesn't sell, we'll discuss whether a price adjustment or different listing strategy makes sense. You are never charged for an item that doesn't sell.
